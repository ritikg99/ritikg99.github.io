Research Assistant - CAMP-PhyRE Lab (University of Washington)

October 2022 – July 2024

I am currently working on a research project focused on the simulation and optimization of an acoustic fish deterrence system deployed within the lock of a Lock and Dam structure. The objective is to optimize the acoustic field generated by a speaker array to deter invasive fish species by minimizing the pressure variance across a region of interest.

My primary task involves optimizing the amplitude and phase settings of the speakers. Since the problem is non-linear and non-convex, I implemented multiple optimization algorithms, including:

  • SLSQP (Sequential Least Squares Quadratic Programming)
  • COBYLA (Constrained Optimization BY Linear Approximation)
  • COBYQA (Constrained Optimization BY Quadratic Approximation)
  • Mini-batch Stochastic Gradient Descent

These algorithms were employed to minimize a custom cost function representing pressure variance, and I successfully achieved a 38% reduction in the cost function.

In addition to optimization, I reconstructed the sound field using Python by post-processing simulation data to compute and visualize the Sound Pressure Level (SPL). This was done for both the baseline and optimized configurations, allowing for comparative analysis of the acoustic field.

All computational work was carried out on the university's High Performance Computing (HPC) cluster, giving me hands-on experience with job scheduling using SLURM, shell scripting, and parallel workflows.
